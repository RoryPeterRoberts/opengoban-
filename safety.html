Safety & Liability

Understanding how Open Gobán works

You are arranging directly with neighbours

When you use Open Gobán, you're making arrangements directly with other people in your community. We're the noticeboard — the exchange happens between you.

What this means

If you agree to help someone fix their fence, or ask for a lift to town, that's an arrangement between the two of you. Open Gobán helps you find each other and keeps track of exchanges, but we're not part of the arrangement itself.

Open Gobán does not provide services or supervision

We are a noticeboard and ledger, not a service provider. We don't:

  • ✗ Provide, deliver, or perform any services
  • ✗ Supervise or check on exchanges
  • ✗ Verify qualifications or run background checks
  • ✗ Guarantee the quality of help offered
  • ✗ Employ or contract anyone

Risk is yours to assess

When you accept help or offer help, you're making a personal judgement. Ask yourself:

  • • Do I feel comfortable with this person?
  • • Does this seem safe to do?
  • • Am I clear on what's being asked or offered?
  • • Would I be comfortable if something went wrong?

Trust your instincts. If something doesn't feel right, don't proceed.

Insurance Note

Important: Open Gobán does not provide any insurance. Exchanges between neighbours are not covered by any policy held by the platform.

If you're concerned about liability for a particular activity, consider:

  • • Your own home insurance (some policies cover volunteer activities)
  • • Personal liability insurance
  • • Discussing the activity scope before starting

High-Risk Activities

Some activities carry more risk than others. Take extra care with:

Ladders & heights Power tools Childcare Eldercare Medical assistance Heavy lifting Driving others Pet care

For these activities, have a clear conversation about experience, expectations, and what happens if something goes wrong. Never feel pressured to do something outside your comfort zone.

Safety Checklist

  1. 1 Agree scope clearly — What exactly are you helping with? What's included and what's not?
  2. 2 Confirm experience — Has the person done this before? Are they comfortable with the task?
  3. 3 Use proper tools — Don't improvise. If you don't have the right equipment, don't proceed.
  4. 4 Stop if unsafe — Either party can stop at any time. Safety comes first.
  5. 5 Keep records — Note what was agreed and what was done. This helps if questions arise later.
  6. 6 Trust your instincts — If something feels wrong, it's okay to say no or stop.
  7. 7 Report concerns — If you have a bad experience, let the community moderators know.
