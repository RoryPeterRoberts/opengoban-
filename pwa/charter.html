Community Charter

How Community Connect is governed

Pilot Programme

Data & Control

Who controls the database? Community Connect is community-governed. The platform operator acts as a custodian — they maintain the technology but don't own or control your community's data.

  • Your data stays local to your community
  • The operator maintains the software but follows community decisions
  • No data is sold or shared with third parties for commercial purposes
  • You can request your data or ask for it to be deleted

Think of it like a village hall: Someone keeps the lights on and the doors open, but the community decides what happens inside.

How Rules Change

Changes to how Community Connect works require community approval. We don't change the rules behind closed doors.

  • Policy proposals are shared with all members
  • Changes require approval from the community panel or a vote
  • All policy changes are logged and visible
  • Members can suggest changes through the feedback system

No Pooled Funds

We avoid pooled money. There's no community fund, no membership fees going into a central pot, and no treasurer controlling cash.

  • Balance credits are not money — they're bookkeeping entries
  • Credits cannot be exchanged for cash
  • No one can "run off with the money" because there is no money
  • All balance changes are logged and transparent

Why this matters: Pooled funds create risk of mismanagement. Our credit system tracks fairness without anyone handling actual money.

Your Data

Your information is kept locally and used only to help the community function.

  • We collect only what's needed: name, contact, exchange history
  • Your data is visible only to other community members
  • We don't share data with advertisers or data brokers
  • You can see and export your own data anytime

Governance Model (Pilot)

  • Moderators are elected or appointed locally by the community
  • Policy changes require approval by the community panel or vote
  • All moderator and admin actions are logged in the audit trail
  • During pilot: the community can revise rules or stop at any time
