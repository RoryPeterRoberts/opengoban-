1. Introduction

What is OpenGoban?

OpenGoban is a community coordination tool that helps groups track mutual support, time contributions, and resource sharing. It operates on the principle of mutual credit: when you help someone in your community, you receive credits; when someone helps you, you give credits.

Unlike conventional money, credits in OpenGoban:

  • Are created through community participation, not by banks or governments
  • Cannot be exchanged for legal tender
  • Have meaning only within your participating community
  • Encourage reciprocity and mutual support

Key Principles

  1. Mutual Credit: The total of all credits in the system always equals zero. When Alice gives 10 credits to Bob, Alice's balance decreases by 10 and Bob's increases by 10.
  2. Credit Limits: Each member has a limit on how far their balance can go negative, preventing runaway debt.
  3. Community Trust: The system works because community members trust each other to contribute back over time.
  4. Privacy First: Your identity is generated locally on your device. No email, password, or government ID required.
  5. Offline Capable: Transfer credits even without internet using QR codes.
Think of it like a favor bank: When you help a neighbor, you "deposit" goodwill. When you need help, you "withdraw." OpenGoban simply keeps track of these contributions in a transparent, fair way.

2. Getting Started

Creating Your Identity

1 Open OpenGoban in your web browser or install the app.
2 Choose a handle (your display name in the community). This could be your first name, nickname, or any identifier you prefer.
3 Tap "Create Identity" - Your cryptographic keys are generated locally on your device. No data is sent to any server.
Important: Your identity exists only on your device. If you clear browser data or lose your device, you will need a backup to recover. See Section 8: Backup & Restore.

Joining a Community (Circle)

After creating your identity, you need to join an existing community or create a new one:

Option A: Get Vouched by an Existing Member

  1. Show your QR code to a current member
  2. They scan it and vouch for you
  3. You're added to the community with an initial credit limit

Option B: Create a New Circle

  1. Go to Settings
  2. Tap "Create Circle"
  3. Invite others by having them scan your QR code

Understanding Your Balance

Your balance can be positive, negative, or zero:

  • Positive balance: You've contributed more than you've received. Others owe the community on your behalf.
  • Zero balance: You've given and received equally.
  • Negative balance: You've received more than you've contributed. You owe contributions back to the community.

Your credit limit is the maximum negative balance allowed. For example, with a limit of 100, your balance can range from -100 to any positive amount.

4. Making Transfers

Sending Credits

1 Tap the Transfer button on the home screen.
2 Scan the recipient's QR code or paste their ID.
3 Enter the amount you want to send.
4 Add a description (optional but recommended) explaining what the credits are for.
5 Confirm the transfer.

Receiving Credits

1 Tap My ID to display your QR code.
2 Have the sender scan your QR code.
3 You'll receive a notification when credits arrive.

Transfer Limits

  • You cannot send credits that would push your balance below your floor (-limit).
  • Both parties must be active members of the community.
  • Transfers are instant and irreversible once confirmed.

5. Commitments & Tasks

What are Commitments?

Commitments are promises to perform tasks or provide services for other community members. They're more formal than simple transfers and can include escrow protection.

Types of Commitments

Type Description Use When
Soft Commitment Trust-based promise, no credits reserved Working with trusted members on small tasks
Escrowed Commitment Credits are reserved until task is complete Larger tasks or working with newer members

Task Categories

Commitments can be organized by essential community needs:

  • Food: Food preparation, cooking, distribution
  • Water & Sanitation: Water management, cleaning
  • Energy & Heat: Energy provision, heating
  • Shelter & Repair: Maintenance, construction, repairs
  • Medical: Healthcare, first aid, wellness
  • Childcare: Dependent care, education
  • Security: Safety coordination, communication
  • Procurement: Supply acquisition, transport
  • General: Other community tasks

Commitment Lifecycle

  1. Create: Promise to do a task for a specific member
  2. Accept: Recipient confirms they want the service
  3. Perform: Complete the promised task
  4. Confirm: Recipient confirms satisfactory completion
  5. Settle: Credits transfer (or escrow releases)

6. Membership & Identity

Member Statuses

Status Meaning Capabilities
Pending Application submitted, waiting for approval None - waiting for vouching
Probation New member with temporary restrictions Limited credit, may require escrowed commitments
Active Full member in good standing All features available
Frozen Temporarily suspended Cannot transact, pending review
Excluded Membership revoked No access

Credit Limits

Each member has a credit limit that determines how far negative their balance can go. Limits are set by community governance based on:

  • Time as a member
  • Track record of fulfilling commitments
  • Community vouching
  • Participation in essential tasks

Sponsorship

When sponsoring a new member:

  • You vouch for their trustworthiness
  • A portion of your credit limit may be "bonded" as guarantee
  • If the new member defaults, you share some responsibility
  • Your bond is released after the probation period if they succeed

7. Offline Mode

Why Offline Mode?

OpenGoban works without internet connectivity. This is useful when:

  • Internet is unavailable or unreliable
  • You want maximum privacy
  • You're in a remote location
  • During emergencies when networks are down

How Offline Transfers Work

Sending Offline

1 Tap Offline on the home screen
2 Choose "Send Credits"
3 Enter amount and description
4 A QR code is generated containing the signed transaction
5 Show this QR code to the recipient

Receiving Offline

1 Tap Offline > "Receive Credits"
2 Scan the sender's QR code
3 The transaction is recorded locally
4 When you're back online, it will sync with the community
Note: Offline transactions are cryptographically signed, ensuring they can't be forged or modified.

8. Backup & Restore

Why Backup?

Your identity exists only on your device. Without a backup, you cannot recover your identity if you:

  • Clear your browser data
  • Lose or replace your device
  • Reinstall the app

Creating a Backup

1 Go to Settings > Backup Identity
2 Enter a strong password (minimum 4 characters, but longer is safer)
3 Confirm the password
4 Tap "Generate Backup"
5 Copy and save the backup code starting with OG1:
Store your backup safely! Anyone with your backup code AND password can access your identity. Store them separately - for example, backup code in a password manager, password memorized or written elsewhere.

Restoring from Backup

1 Go to Settings > Restore from Backup
2 Paste your backup code (the OG1:... string)
3 Enter your backup password
4 Tap "Restore Identity"
Warning: Restoring will replace any existing identity on the device.

9. Community Governance

How Decisions Are Made

OpenGoban communities make collective decisions through a governance system. Major decisions require proposals and voting.

Types of Proposals

  • Member Admission: Approve new members
  • Member Exclusion: Remove problematic members
  • Credit Limit Changes: Adjust member limits
  • Dispute Resolution: Settle conflicts between members
  • Parameter Changes: Adjust community settings
  • Emergency Declaration: Activate emergency protocols

Voting

  • Most decisions require a simple majority (>50%)
  • Critical decisions (exclusion, emergency) may require supermajority (>66%)
  • Members can vote Yes, No, or Abstain
  • A minimum number of votes (quorum) is required for validity

Disputes

If you have a dispute with another member:

  1. Try to resolve it directly first
  2. If unsuccessful, file a formal dispute with evidence
  3. The community reviews and votes on resolution
  4. Decisions can be appealed in serious cases

10. Multi-Community Federation

What is Federation?

Federation allows separate communities (cells) to trade with each other while maintaining their independence.

How It Works

  • Communities establish bilateral links
  • Members can transact across community boundaries
  • Each community sets exposure limits to manage risk
  • If one community has problems, others are protected

Exposure Limits

To prevent one community's problems from affecting others:

  • Position Cap: Maximum net balance with any single community
  • Quarantine: Automatic isolation if limits are exceeded
  • Beta Factor: Risk weighting based on community health

11. Emergency Mode

Risk States

Communities operate in one of three states:

State Meaning Automatic Changes
Normal Healthy operation All features available
Stressed Warning indicators elevated Tighter admission, encourage escrowed commitments
Panic Critical emergency Essential tasks only, restricted admission, mandatory escrow

Stress Indicators

The system monitors:

  • Floor Mass: How many members are near their debt limit
  • Balance Variance: How unequal balances have become
  • Dispute Rate: Frequency of conflicts
  • Member Churn: Rate of members leaving
  • Resource Stress: Shortages in essential categories

What to Do in an Emergency

  1. Check the current risk state in your app
  2. Prioritize essential community needs (food, water, medical, energy)
  3. Use escrowed commitments for all significant tasks
  4. Coordinate with community leadership
  5. Help vulnerable members

12. Best Practices

For Individual Members

  • Back up your identity immediately after creating it
  • Add descriptions to all transfers to maintain a clear record
  • Keep your balance near zero over time - both large positive and negative balances indicate imbalance
  • Fulfill commitments promptly and completely
  • Participate in governance - vote on proposals
  • Report issues early rather than letting them escalate

For Community Organizers

  • Start small - begin with people who already trust each other
  • Set reasonable credit limits - too high creates risk, too low limits usefulness
  • Establish clear norms about what credits represent
  • Have regular check-ins to discuss community health
  • Document decisions and their rationale
  • Plan for emergencies before they happen

Security Tips

  • Never share your backup code publicly
  • Verify recipients before sending credits
  • Use a strong backup password
  • Be cautious of strangers asking for credits
  • Report suspicious activity to community governance

13. Troubleshooting

Common Issues

"Transfer failed - insufficient balance"

Your balance would go below your floor (-limit). Either:

  • Send a smaller amount
  • Wait until you receive credits
  • Request a limit increase from governance

"Cannot find member"

  • Ensure you're scanning a valid OpenGoban QR code
  • Check that you're both in the same community
  • Try pasting their ID manually

"Sync failed"

  • Check your internet connection
  • Verify the sync URL is correct
  • Try disconnecting and reconnecting

"Lost my identity"

  • If you have a backup, restore from it
  • If not, you'll need to create a new identity and get re-vouched
  • Your old balance cannot be recovered without your keys

"QR code not scanning"

  • Ensure good lighting
  • Hold the camera steady
  • Move closer or further from the code
  • Clean your camera lens
