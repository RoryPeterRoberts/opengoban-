TechnoCommune Mutual Aid System — Mathematical Model (v0.1)
1) Entities and state
Let there be a circle with members i \in \{1,\dots,N\}, plus one special community account G (“Guarantee / Commons Pool”).
Balances
Each member has a credit balance:
b_i \in \mathbb{Z} \quad (\text{credits})
Community pool has:
b_G \in \mathbb{Z}
Invariant: conservation
For a pure mutual-credit system:
\sum_{i=1}^{N} b_i + b_G = 0
This is the key “not a crypto scam” property: no hidden issuance. Any “support” is a reallocation of claims/obligations inside the circle.
________________


2) Day-to-day trading (normal mode)
A transaction t from sender s to recipient r of amount a>0 updates balances:
b_s \leftarrow b_s - a,\quad b_r \leftarrow b_r + a
Credit limits (trust boundary)
Each member has a floor L_i \le 0 (how far negative they may go):
b_i \ge L_i
Optionally a positive cap U_i to prevent hoarding/liquidity lock:
b_i \le U_i
(You might not enforce U_i early; it’s a governance knob.)
So a normal transaction is valid iff:
b_s - a \ge L_s \quad \text{and (optional) } b_r + a \le U_r
________________


3) Crisis provisioning (survival mode) without fiat and without breaking conservation
You want: “If I lose my job, I can still eat / stay warm / get help.”
If you let providers take all the risk, the system burns out. So you introduce community underwriting explicitly via G.
The essential move: tri-party clearing
For an essential good/service provided by p to a person in crisis c, amount a:
1. Pay the provider from the community pool

b_G \leftarrow b_G - a,\quad b_p \leftarrow b_p + a

2. Record the recipient’s obligation to the pool (not to the provider)

You can do this as an immediate counter-transfer (or a linked obligation object):

b_c \leftarrow b_c - a,\quad b_G \leftarrow b_G + a

If you do both steps immediately, it collapses back to “provider paid by recipient.”

So instead, the better structure is:

   * Provider gets paid now (step 1).

   * Recipient accrues a separate obligation o_c to the pool that is repaid later with labor/credits when possible.

So you maintain two ledgers:
      * Balance ledger b (mutual credit, sums to zero)

      * Obligation ledger o (a non-transferable “I owe the community” record)

That gives you crisis support without forcing providers to accept negative-risk individuals directly.
Pool capacity constraint (prevents collapse)
Pool has a floor:
b_G \ge L_G \quad (L_G < 0)
This is the hard cap on total outstanding crisis support.
Interpretation: -L_G is the community’s maximum collective risk appetite.
You can set:
L_G = -\alpha N
where \alpha is “credits of safety net per person” (governance choice).
________________


4) Repayment and sustainability (still no fiat)
To replenish the pool (bring b_G back toward 0), members contribute credits through normal transactions:
If member i contributes x:
b_i \leftarrow b_i - x,\quad b_G \leftarrow b_G + x
Where do these credits come from? From the fact that members with positive balances are holding claims on community goods/services. Contributing to G is just saying: “I’ll convert some of my claim into shared resilience.”
You can source contributions via policy knobs that remain “local-community, not cabal”:
         * Voluntary contributions

         * Membership dues in credits (tiny recurring)

         * Demurrage on excess positive balances (optional, only on balances above a threshold)

         * Project rewards (community work earns credits from G up to caps)

All of these preserve \sum b = 0. No minting required.
________________


5) Trust and limits: a math function you can tune
You already have a time ladder. To make it Sybil-resistant and community-real, make the limit a function:
L_i = -\min\Big(L_{\max},\; f(\text{tenure}) + g(\text{integration}) + h(\text{reliability}) - p(\text{disputes})\Big)
Concrete example (simple, robust):
            * Tenure component: f = \text{step ladder}

            * Integration: g = k \cdot \sqrt{u_i} where u_i = number of unique trade partners outside one’s vouch cluster

            * Reliability: h = m \cdot (\text{commitment\_completion\_rate})

            * Penalty: p = d \cdot \text{open\_disputes} + n \cdot \text{no\_shows}

This keeps the “math” legible while aligning with your values: real participation grows trust, not performative posting.
________________


6) What this buys you conceptually
               * Day-to-day barter + services work with standard mutual credit.

               * Crisis provisioning becomes a bounded, explicit social safety net:

                  * providers get paid reliably

                  * recipients don’t get ostracized

                  * community risk is capped by L_G

                     * No hidden issuance: the system doesn’t become “fake money.” It’s claims and obligations inside a real community capacity.

Both are workable, but they imply different math for pool depletion and replenishment.

Crisis Provisioning Math (Model A with Compassion Override)
1) Two ledgers: balances and obligations
Mutual-credit balances (transferable, conserved)
                           * Member balances: b_i

                           * Community pool balance: b_G

Invariant:
\sum_{i=1}^{N} b_i + b_G = 0
Crisis obligations (non-transferable, owed to the community)
Each member may also have an obligation balance:
o_i \ge 0
Interpretation: “I received essential support worth o_i credits and I owe the community back over time when able.”
Obligations do not break conservation because they’re tracked separately; they’re a commitment record, not spendable money.
________________


2) Crisis transaction: provider gets paid, recipient accrues obligation
When provider p supplies essential value a to crisis recipient c:
Step 1 — Pay the provider from the pool
b_p \leftarrow b_p + a,\quad b_G \leftarrow b_G - a
Step 2 — Record the recipient’s obligation
o_c \leftarrow o_c + a
That’s it. No debit to b_c. This is the key: the provider is not taking counterparty risk; the community pool is.
________________


3) Repayment: obligations convert into credits flowing back to the pool
When recipient c later contributes repayment x (through work, goods, services sold for credits, etc.):
                              * They must actually earn credits in normal trades, raising b_c.

                              * Repayment is a transfer to the pool:

b_c \leftarrow b_c - x,\quad b_G \leftarrow b_G + x,\quad o_c \leftarrow o_c - x
Constraints:
0 \le x \le o_c
This makes repayment mechanically identical to “donating credits to the community,” except it also reduces the tracked obligation.
________________


4) Pool solvency: the hard cap that prevents runaway “free essentials”
Define a pool credit limit:
b_G \ge L_G \quad\text{where } L_G < 0
This is the maximum collective crisis provisioning capacity:
\text{Max outstanding support} \approx -L_G
Operational rule: an essential-provisioning payment of amount a is allowed only if:
b_G - a \ge L_G
You can set:
L_G = -\alpha N
where \alpha is “credits of safety net per member” (policy choice).
________________


5) Compassion override: converting obligation into community-absorbed loss
Sometimes repayment will never happen (illness, disability, death, long-term incapacity). You want a dignified override.
Define a governance action “Compassion Forgiveness” that writes off y credits of obligation for member c:
o_c \leftarrow o_c - y
But forgiveness must have an accounting consequence or you silently create unbacked claims. The clean way is to recognize it as a community loss.
Introduce a non-person entity L (“Loss Sink / Socialized Loss Account”) with a balance b_L that is allowed to go negative:
                                 * Add it to the conservation invariant:

\sum_{i=1}^{N} b_i + b_G + b_L = 0

When you forgive y, you move that cost to the loss sink:
b_L \leftarrow b_L - y
Interpretation: the community has decided “we will absorb this.” No individual gets debited; the cost is carried collectively and can be repaired later by future contributions (bringing b_L back toward 0).
Loss budget (prevents moral hazard)
Set a bound:
b_L \ge L_L \quad (L_L < 0)
so there is a maximum compassion capacity over a time horizon.
This is your “we are compassionate but not suicidal” knob.
________________


6) Eligibility: who can draw from crisis provisioning?
This is policy, but you can encode it as constraints to keep the math stable.
Category caps (Essentials only)
Crisis spending only allowed for categories E: food, heat, transport-to-care, basic repairs, care work.
Individual draw cap
Per member, per time window T:
\sum_{t \in T} a_t \le C_T
Example: C_{\text{week}} = 20 credits; C_{\text{month}} = 60.
“Ability” flag for forgiveness consideration
Forgiveness is only considered when:
                                    * verified incapacity flag is set (by governance), or

                                    * obligation is older than some threshold and member has had zero earning capacity indicators for a long period.

________________


7) What this implies for your existing “forgiveness” code
Your current “mint + cancel” approach effectively dilutes silently. Replace it with:
                                       * Repayable path: obligations tracked; pool pays providers; repayment flows back.

                                       * Forgiveness path: reduce obligation and push cost into Loss Sink b_L, not minting.

This keeps the whole system auditable and preserves the “not a scam” property: every act of compassion is visible as a real community decision with a bounded budget.
________________


8) One sentence summary of the model
Day-to-day trade is mutual credit; crisis provisioning is pool-underwritten essentials that create a repayable obligation; compassion override writes off obligations and books the cost explicitly into a bounded communal loss account.

Parameterization and Stability (v0.2)
This section picks default numbers that make the system (a) useful in day-to-day life, (b) able to absorb short-term job-loss shocks, and (c) hard to collapse via generosity or freeloading.
________________


1) Choose units so caps are intuitive
Pick 1 credit ≈ 10 minutes of ordinary local labour as a starting convention (not a hard peg; just a shared mental model).
Then:
                                          * dog walk (30–40 min) ≈ 3–4 CR

                                          * grass cut (60–90 min) ≈ 6–9 CR

                                          * a bag of veg ≈ 2–5 CR

                                          * beach clean (2 hours) ≈ 12 CR (but usually handled as “project gratitude” + optional pool rewards)

This makes “weekly essential cap” easy to set.
________________


2) Core limits to set on Day 1
(A) Member credit limit ladder (day-to-day mutual credit)
Keep your time ladder, but add one integration condition (cheap Sybil hardening):
L_i(\text{tenure}) \in \{0,-10,-25,-50,-100\}
Default ladder (your current):
                                             * 0 months: L=0

                                             * 1 month: L=-10

                                             * 3 months: L=-25

                                             * 6 months: L=-50

                                             * 12 months: L=-100

Add one rule: limit only steps up if member has at least
u_i \ge u_{\min}
unique trade partners (outside their own vouch cluster). Defaults:
                                                * to get -10: u_{\min}=3

                                                * to get -25: u_{\min}=6

                                                * to get -50: u_{\min}=10

                                                * to get -100: u_{\min}=15

This keeps the ladder “human scale” and makes Sybil farming much harder.
________________


(B) Community pool limit L_G (crisis provisioning capacity)
Let the pool’s max deficit be proportional to community size:
L_G = -\alpha N
Good starting value: \alpha = 20 credits/member.
Interpretation: the circle can float about 200 minutes of labour per member worth of essentials during a crisis wave.
Examples:
                                                   * N=50 \Rightarrow L_G=-1000

                                                   * N=100 \Rightarrow L_G=-2000

This is meaningful but not infinite.
________________


(C) Compassion loss budget L_L (write-offs for illness/disability)
Set a smaller bound than the pool:
L_L = -\beta N
Good starting value: \beta = 5 credits/member.
Examples:
                                                      * N=50 \Rightarrow L_L=-250

                                                      * N=100 \Rightarrow L_L=-500

This means: compassion exists, but it’s a scarce communal resource that must be renewed.
________________


3) Crisis provisioning caps (so it helps, but doesn’t become a black hole)
Essentials-only categories
Allow crisis support only for a fixed allowlist E:
Food, heat, transport-to-care, basic home repair, care work, hygiene basics.
Individual draw caps
You need two caps: a short-term cap and a rolling cap.
                                                         * Weekly cap: C_{\text{week}} = 20 credits

                                                         * Monthly cap: C_{\text{month}} = 60 credits

This is enough to prevent hunger/cold in a functioning circle, while forcing prioritization.
Crisis duration (default)
Crisis mode is time-boxed unless renewed:
                                                            * Default crisis window: 8 weeks

                                                            * Renewal requires steward review (lightweight)

________________


4) Repayment rule (simple, humane, non-punitive)
When someone in crisis earns credits again, repayment should be automatic but gentle.
Define an auto-repay fraction:
\rho \in [0,1]
On every incoming normal payment to member c of amount a, automatically route:
x = \min(\rho a,\; o_c)
to the pool (repayment), leaving a-x for the member to live on.
Good default: \rho = 0.2 (20%)
This prevents “debt trap” dynamics while ensuring the pool refills over time.
________________


5) Provider protection (prevents burnout)
Providers should never have to wonder if “helping in crisis” will leave them holding the bag.
Rule: all crisis provisioning pays providers from G immediately, subject to b_G \ge L_G.
Additionally, add a provider-side throttle:
                                                               * Any one provider can receive at most P_{\text{week}} = 100 crisis-paid credits/week

(prevents one person becoming the “essential services slave” for the whole circle).

________________


6) Stability conditions (what must be true for this to not grind to a halt)
This is the real math: the pool can only function if inflows roughly match outflows over time.
Let:
                                                                  * S(t) = crisis support paid out by pool per month

                                                                  * R(t) = repayments to pool per month (including voluntary contributions)

                                                                  * \Delta b_G = R(t) - S(t)

For sustainability you need:
\mathbb{E}[R(t)] \gtrsim \mathbb{E}[S(t)]
and you must maintain:
b_G \ge L_G
Your knobs to maintain this:
                                                                     * reduce caps C_{\text{week}}, C_{\text{month}} if pool drains

                                                                     * adjust \rho upward slightly if pool doesn’t refill (e.g., 0.2 → 0.25)

                                                                     * encourage non-crisis members with high positive balances to contribute voluntarily (or add mild demurrage only above a high threshold)

________________


7) Worked example (small circle, one job-loss event)
Assume:
                                                                        * N=50

                                                                        * L_G=-1000 (α=20)

                                                                        * Weekly cap = 20

                                                                        * \rho=0.2

Person c loses job, enters crisis mode for 8 weeks.
They draw average 15 credits/week in essentials (food + heat help + transport).
Total support:
S = 8 \times 15 = 120
Pool goes:
b_G \leftarrow b_G - 120
Obligation:
o_c \leftarrow o_c + 120
Later, c starts earning by doing community tasks and small jobs:
                                                                           * earns 30 credits/month net inflow via normal trades

Auto-repay at 20%:
R \approx 0.2 \times 30 = 6 \text{ credits/month}
Time to repay 120 credits at 6/month is ~20 months — which is slow but humane. If they earn more, it repays faster; if not, it doesn’t crush them.
If c becomes permanently ill and can’t repay, governance may forgive e.g. y=60:
o_c \leftarrow o_c - 60,\quad b_L \leftarrow b_L - 60
No one else’s balance changes; the community simply records that it absorbed that cost.
________________


8) Operational monitoring (your “dashboard math”)
You only need a few numbers to keep this healthy:
                                                                              * Pool health: b_G and distance to limit (b_G - L_G)

                                                                              * Monthly support flow: S(t)

                                                                              * Monthly repayment flow: R(t)

                                                                              * Outstanding obligations: \sum_i o_i

                                                                              * Compassion usage: b_L and distance to limit (b_L - L_L)

                                                                              * Burnout risk: top 10 providers’ crisis-paid totals (concentration)

If any metric trends badly, you adjust caps/ρ, not by central authority fiat, but by community-agreed parameters.
________________


9) Document clause you should write explicitly (anti-scam, anti-cabal)
                                                                                 1. No hidden minting. All balances conserve to zero including governance accounts.

                                                                                 2. Crisis help is a community decision with bounded capacity.

                                                                                 3. Compassion is explicit and budgeted. Write-offs are visible and capped.

                                                                                 4. The goal is provisioning and resilience, not speculation and extraction.

2) Validation engine rules (deterministic, post-merge)
Rule 0: Schema + size gate (anti-corruption baseline)
Reject/ignore any doc that:
                                                                                                                        * has unknown type

                                                                                                                        * exceeds size limit (e.g., 32KB)

                                                                                                                        * is missing required fields

                                                                                                                        * fails signature checks

This is your minimal “Storage Bomb” defense even for friendly communities.
________________


3) Balance computation (single source of truth)
Compute balances from confirmed documents only:
Let accounts include members + G + LOSS.
                                                                                                                           * For each confirmed transfer:

                                                                                                                              * sender −amount, recipient +amount

                                                                                                                                 * For each confirmed crisis_payment:

                                                                                                                                    * G −amount, provider +amount

                                                                                                                                       * For each confirmed repayment:

                                                                                                                                          * member −amount, G +amount

                                                                                                                                             * For each executed compassion_forgiveness:

                                                                                                                                                * LOSS −amount

(No balance movement for the forgiven member; only obligations change.)

Then check conservation:
\sum b = 0
If not zero, you have corruption or a bug (or a transaction type that isn’t accounted for).
________________


4) Obligation computation
Obligations derive from:
                                                                                                                                                   * +amount for each confirmed crisis_payment where member is recipient

                                                                                                                                                   * −amount for each confirmed repayment by member

                                                                                                                                                   * −amount for each executed compassion_forgiveness on member

Invariant:
o_i \ge 0
If negative due to race/ordering, clamp by rejecting the over-repayment / over-forgiveness at validation.
________________


5) Transaction validity conditions
A) Normal transfer validity
A confirmed transfer a is valid iff:
                                                                                                                                                      1. signatures valid

                                                                                                                                                      2. idempotency: canonical_hash not previously applied

                                                                                                                                                      3. sender not frozen/revoked

                                                                                                                                                      4. post-application sender respects limit:

b_s - a \ge L_s

(Enforced deterministically after merge; breaches → freeze account + mark offending tx as “breach-causing” if you want auditability.)

Optional:
b_r + a \le U_r
________________


B) Crisis payment validity
Confirmed crisis payment a is valid iff:
                                                                                                                                                         1. category in allowlist E

                                                                                                                                                         2. provider acceptance signature present

                                                                                                                                                         3. pool capacity:

b_G - a \ge L_G

                                                                                                                                                         4. recipient crisis cap not exceeded in time window:

\sum_{t \in \text{week}} a_t \le C_{\text{week}},\quad \sum_{t \in \text{month}} a_t \le C_{\text{month}}

                                                                                                                                                         5. recipient status not revoked/anonymized in a way that disallows provisioning (policy)

                                                                                                                                                         6. idempotency (hash)

If any fail: mark doc rejected (don’t apply effects).
________________


C) Repayment validity
Confirmed repayment x is valid iff:
                                                                                                                                                            1. payer signature valid

                                                                                                                                                            2. payer not frozen/revoked (policy choice: allow frozen to repay is often useful)

                                                                                                                                                            3. payer has sufficient balance and limit:

b_c - x \ge L_c

                                                                                                                                                            4. obligation sufficiency:

o_c - x \ge 0

________________


D) Compassion forgiveness validity
Executable forgiveness y is valid iff:
                                                                                                                                                               1. elder signatures ≥ threshold

                                                                                                                                                               2. timelock satisfied (now ≥ timelock_until)

                                                                                                                                                               3. no community veto present (if implemented)

                                                                                                                                                               4. loss budget not exceeded:

b_L - y \ge L_L

                                                                                                                                                               5. obligation sufficiency:

o_c - y \ge 0

                                                                                                                                                                           5. Compassion override governance flow (timelock, signatures, execution)

Workflow Specifications (v0.4)
These flows are written to be Maslow-first, low-friction for older users, and anti-Facebook (coordination over posting). Each flow ends in deterministic ledger objects from v0.3.
________________


1) Day-to-day: “Grass needs cutting” (normal mode)
Goal: Mary (elderly) gets her grass cut this week. Seán earns credits or swaps value.
                                                                                                                                                                              1. Mary posts a Need

                                                                                                                                                                                 * Screen: “Request Help”

                                                                                                                                                                                 * Picks template: Garden → Grass cutting

                                                                                                                                                                                 * Sets: time window (e.g., “this week”), location radius, optional note (“front only”)

                                                                                                                                                                                 * Chooses settlement: Credits (default), Swap, or “Ask first”

                                                                                                                                                                                 * App creates: need_<uuid> (non-ledger coordination object)

                                                                                                                                                                                    2. Helpers see it

                                                                                                                                                                                       * Seán opens Nearby Needs

                                                                                                                                                                                       * Filters: “Essentials / Elder support” (optional)

                                                                                                                                                                                       * Taps I can help

                                                                                                                                                                                          3. Commitment proposed

                                                                                                                                                                                             * Seán selects a time slot

                                                                                                                                                                                             * App creates: commitment_<uuid> with:

                                                                                                                                                                                                * need_id

                                                                                                                                                                                                * helper_id

                                                                                                                                                                                                * proposed time

                                                                                                                                                                                                * status proposed

                                                                                                                                                                                                   4. Mary accepts (two taps)

                                                                                                                                                                                                      * Mary sees: “Seán can cut grass Friday 11:00”

                                                                                                                                                                                                      * Taps Accept

                                                                                                                                                                                                      * Commitment status → scheduled

                                                                                                                                                                                                         5. Completion

                                                                                                                                                                                                            * Seán marks Done

                                                                                                                                                                                                            * Mary confirms Done (or steward confirms if Mary struggles)

                                                                                                                                                                                                            * Commitment status → completed

                                                                                                                                                                                                               6. Settlement

                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * If in normal mode: app prompts “Pay Seán: [6 CR]”

                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * Mary taps Pay (or steward assists)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * Creates tx transfer Mary → Seán, amount a

                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * If Mary has insufficient balance and no crisis status: app suggests swap or partial payment

Ledger outcome: confirmed tx transfer.
________________


2) Day-to-day: “Vegetable swap” (no credits or optional credits)
Goal: Aoife has surplus carrots; Tom has surplus eggs; they swap.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                     1. Aoife posts Offer: “Carrots, 2kg”

                                                                                                                                                                                                                     2. Tom taps Swap and proposes “6 eggs” (or “3 CR”)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                     3. They meet and confirm exchange

                                                                                                                                                                                                                     4. Settlement:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * If pure swap: no ledger entry (optional “thank you” record for reputation reliability)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * If credits used: one tx transfer for agreed amount

Ledger outcome: optional tx or no ledger (policy choice).
________________


3) Day-to-day: “Dog walking” (recurring commitment)
Goal: Reliable routine support without constant posting.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                           1. Owner posts Need: “Dog walk Tue/Thu mornings”

                                                                                                                                                                                                                           2. Helper proposes recurring schedule

                                                                                                                                                                                                                           3. Owner accepts → recurring commitment series

                                                                                                                                                                                                                           4. Each walk: tap Done/Confirm

                                                                                                                                                                                                                           5. Settlement:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Batch settlement weekly (recommended): one payment for the week

                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Avoid per-walk micro-payments unless necessary

Ledger outcome: periodic tx transfers, lower friction.
________________


4) Public good: “Beach clean” (project mode, not credit farming)
Goal: Organize cleanup without turning it into status or arbitrage.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 1. Steward posts Project: “Beach Clean Saturday 10:00–12:00”

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 2. People join → project_participant records

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 3. On day: check-in via steward (simple list) or QR at location

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 4. Completion:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    * Participants get a non-tradable gratitude marker (reputation: “shows up”)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    * Optional: small credit reward paid from pool G but capped:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       * e.g., 5 CR/person max

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       * total project budget fixed (e.g., 100 CR)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       * prevents “farming”

If you do pay credits:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          * Create crisis_payment-like object but for project_reward type, paid from a community budget account (still conserved; still capped).

Ledger outcome: ideally none; optionally controlled community-budget payouts.

Crisis Mode Workflows
5) Entering Crisis Mode (job loss)
Goal: Dignity-first access to essentials without forcing providers to take risk.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             1. Member toggles “I’m in crisis” (private)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             2. App prompts minimal reason category (job loss/illness/other) + expected duration

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             3. Request goes to Stewards/Elders (not the whole community)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             4. Steward approves:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                * sets member.crisis_status = active

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                * sets an expiry date (default 8 weeks)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                * optionally sets category allowances (food/heat/transport)

Result: member can request essentials under caps.
________________


6) Crisis provisioning: “Need food and firewood this week”
Flow A — Recipient creates an Essential Need
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   1. Recipient posts Need in Essentials category

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      * It is shown to providers as “Community-supported” (no stigma wording)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         2. Provider accepts and schedules as normal (commitment)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         3. On completion, settlement screen shows:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            * “Paid by Community Pool” (not by recipient)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            * Amount suggested from template or negotiated

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               4. Provider taps Confirm payout

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * App creates crisis_payment:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     * recipient_id = crisis member

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     * provider_id = provider

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     * amount a

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     * category in allowlist

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        5. Validation checks (post-sync if needed):

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           * pool capacity b_G - a \ge L_G

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           * recipient weekly/monthly caps

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           * category allowlist

If passes → confirmed:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * provider credited (+a)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * pool debited (−a)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * recipient obligation o_c increases (+a)

If fails → rejected:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 * app suggests smaller amount or delays until pool refills

________________


7) Auto-repay behavior (gentle repayment when able)
Whenever the crisis member later receives normal income (credits) via standard trades:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    * system routes fraction \rho (default 20%) automatically into repayment transfers to G until o_c is cleared.

User experience:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       * they see “Repayment: 2 CR to Community (20%)” on each incoming payment (transparent, not punitive)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       * they can pause repayment in emergencies (policy-controlled; stewards can override)

Compassion Override Workflow
8) Compassion write-off (illness, disability, death)
Goal: absorb non-repayable obligations transparently and with limits.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          1. Steward proposes compassion_forgiveness:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             * member_id

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             * amount y

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             * reason code

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             * optional evidence hashes

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             * timelock set (e.g., 7 days)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                2. Elders sign (multi-sig threshold)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                3. Proposal is visible in Governance Log (redacted details)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                4. During timelock:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * community can veto if your model includes veto

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      5. After timelock, execute:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * obligation reduces by y

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * loss sink b_L becomes more negative by y

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * no one else’s balance changes

If loss budget would be breached (b_L - y < L_L):
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            * execution blocked → forces community to replenish/decide priorities

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               * provider weekly cap

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               * steward review renewal

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               * dispute/report mechanism

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               * temporary suspension from crisis payouts (without revoking normal participation)

Frozen member can still:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * receive credits

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * repay obligations

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * request essentials only via steward discretion (policy choice)

Frozen member cannot:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     * send normal transfers

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     * create new commitments beyond low-risk categories (optional)

Circle Policy Defaults (v0.6)
This section defines default settings for a new community circle that optimize for: survival provisioning, low toxicity, Sybil resistance through human trust, and smooth day-to-day trade.
________________


1) Membership and trust (onboarding)
1.1 Join requirements
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               * min_vouchers = 1 for first 50 members

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               * min_vouchers = 2 after 50 members (optional step-up)

Rationale: keeps onboarding relational without becoming bureaucratic.
1.2 Bootstrap mode (new circle cold start)
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * Circle creator becomes active elder automatically.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * First 5 members require only creator vouch.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * After 5 members: normal vouch rules apply.

1.3 One human, one account (social rule)
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     * Policy statement (not tech): one person should have one identity in the circle.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     * Enforcement: steward/elder discretion; later you can add optional device warnings.

________________


2) Normal mutual credit parameters
2.1 Credit unit convention
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * Soft norm: 1 CR ≈ 10 minutes of ordinary local labour

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * Not enforced; only used for templates and suggested pricing.

2.2 Circuit breaker
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           * MAX_TRANSACTION = 100 CR

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           * Additional: “Large transfer confirm” prompt at 20+ CR

Rationale: prevents fat-finger disasters and reduces coercion potential.
2.3 Credit limit progression (day-to-day)
Default ladder (as you already have), but with integration gate:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Month 0: credit_limit = 0

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Month 1: -10 if unique_trade_partners >= 3

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Month 3: -25 if >= 6

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Month 6: -50 if >= 10

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Month 12: -100 if >= 15

Rationale: time alone is Sybil-friendly; unique partners makes trust “real.”
2.4 Account freeze rule
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 * If post-merge validation finds b_i < L_i → status=frozen

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 * Frozen accounts:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    * can receive credits

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    * can repay obligations

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    * cannot send normal transfers

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    * crisis access only via steward discretion (see §4)

________________


3) Privacy defaults
3.1 Transaction privacy
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       * Default: only counterparties can see transaction details.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       * Public: only minimal reliability metrics (see §6).

3.2 Location privacy
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          * Need posts show approximate location (radius) until a commitment is accepted.

3.3 Messaging
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             * Messaging is task-bound by default.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             * No public comment threads.

________________


4) Crisis provisioning defaults (survival net)
4.1 Eligibility and activation
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                * Crisis mode is steward-approved, not self-asserted to the whole circle.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                * Default duration: 8 weeks

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                * Renewal: steward review every 8 weeks

4.2 Essentials categories allowlist E
Enabled by default:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Food

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Heat

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Transport to care/work search

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Basic home repair (safety-critical)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Care work (elder support, childcare, disability support)

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Hygiene basics

Disabled by default:
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      * luxury categories

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      * large durable goods

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      * anything that looks like arbitrage

4.3 Individual caps
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * Weekly cap: C_week = 20 CR

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * Monthly cap: C_month = 60 CR
